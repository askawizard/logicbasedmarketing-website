Las Vegas Businesses That Are Leaving Money on the Table

Las Vegas has one of the most concentrated collections of founder-led $3M–$10M businesses in the country. They're in four industries, and every one of them has the same data problem.

Hospitality Supply & Services

Companies supplying or servicing hotels and casinos. High revenue, thin margins, phone-heavy sales with zero attribution. If you're doing $3M–$10M supplying to the strip, you have a data problem. You're making deals, but can you tell which ones are profitable?

Medical Aesthetics & Dental

Med spas, plastic surgery groups, multi-location dental chains. You're spending $800–$1,500 per new patient. Do you actually know which campaigns are driving that? Most don't. You're scaling marketing without knowing your real unit economics.

Cannabis Operators

Nevada was early. The operators who survived to 2026 figured out compliance but still have patchwork data infrastructure. High margin on some products, terrible margin on others. You often can't tell which is which until quarter-end.

Construction & Specialty Trades

HVAC, plumbing, electrical doing $3M–$10M. Job costing is notoriously broken. You're winning bids and losing money and it doesn't show up until quarter-end. You're cash-flowing a business you think is profitable.
