Marketing Attribution Statistics 2026: 38 Stats on Multi-Touch, Data Gaps, and Revenue Impact

Tyler Sass February 25, 2026 10 min read

Your ad platform says one thing. Your CRM says something different. Your spreadsheet says something else entirely. That's attribution—and for most businesses, it's the single biggest source of invisible revenue loss.

I've compiled 38 marketing attribution statistics from research firms, practitioner surveys, and platform studies. Every stat is sourced. Whether you're making the case for better attribution internally, benchmarking your own setup, or just trying to understand how widespread this problem is these numbers tell the story.

The Attribution Problem

1. 72% of marketers say they cannot accurately measure the ROI of their marketing spend across all channels (Forrester Research 2023)

2. Only 23% of B2B companies are confident that their attribution data is accurate (Demand Gen Report, 2022)

3. The average B2B buyer interacts with 10 or more marketing touchpoints before purchasing and most attribution models track fewer than 3 (Gartner)

4. 87% of marketing executives say data quality issues directly impact their revenue decisions (Salesforce State of Marketing 2023)

5. 58% of marketers still use last-click attribution as their primary model despite widespread acknowledgment that it's inaccurate (HubSpot State of Marketing 2023)

6. Businesses using last-click attribution overvalue paid search by an average of 94% and undervalue email by 75% (Google/Ipsos Attribution Research)

Ad platform-reported CAC is on average 5-15x lower than actual blended CAC when all costs are factored in.

McKinsey Digital

Multi-Touch Attribution Adoption

7. Only 18% of companies have implemented multi-touch attribution across all marketing channels (Forrester Wave on Attribution, 2022)

8. Companies using multi-touch attribution report 15-20% improvement in marketing ROI compared to last-click models (Forrester)

9. 61% of marketers say their biggest barrier to multi-touch attribution is data integration across platforms (Salesforce)

10. Multi-touch attribution adoption is highest in enterprise companies ($500M+ revenue) at 42% and lowest in SMBs under $20M at 8% (Aberdeen Group)

11. Companies that switch from single-touch to multi-touch attribution reallocate an average of 30% of their marketing budget within the first 6 months (MarTech Alliance)

12. 79% of marketers who have implemented multi-touch attribution say it changed which channels they invest in (Demand Gen Report)

Companies using multi-touch attribution report 15-20% improvement in marketing ROI compared to last-click models.

Forrester

Platform Reporting vs. Reality

13. Ad platform-reported customer acquisition costs are on average 5-15x lower than actual blended CAC when all costs are factored in (McKinsey Digital)

14. Facebook Ads and Google Ads both take credit for the same conversion in 35-40% of multi-channel customer journeys (Nielsen Digital Ad Ratings research)

15. Self-reported attribution from ad platforms overstates their contribution to revenue by an average of 77% (Recast Media Mix Modeling Study, 2022)

16. 64% of performance marketers say they have caught at least one major platform attribution error in the last 12 months (Performance Marketing World survey)

17. The average e-commerce brand running ads on 3+ platforms has those platforms collectively claiming 140-200% of actual conversions (Triple Whale / Northbeam industry analysis)

18. iOS 14.5+ (2021) caused reported Facebook conversion data to decline 15-30% for most advertisers, while actual conversions remained stable revealing how much platform tracking inflated historical numbers (Meta Business Help / Measured.com)

Revenue Impact of Poor Attribution

19. Companies with poor attribution make budget decisions based on the wrong channels losing an average of 25-30% of marketing budget effectiveness annually (McKinsey)

20. 42% of companies killed a marketing channel in the last 12 months based on attribution data that later turned out to be wrong (Demand Gen Report 2023)

21. Poor attribution costs the average $10M-$50M business between $400K and $2M per year in misallocated marketing spend (Forrester SMB Analysis)

22. Businesses that improve attribution accuracy report average CAC reductions of 18-35% within 90 days of implementation (Marketing Attribution Association)

23. 1 in 3 SaaS companies have at one point completely misattributed their fastest-growing acquisition channel (OpenView Partners SaaS Benchmarks)

24. Companies with disconnected attribution see 2.3x higher customer acquisition costs than companies with unified attribution across channels (Aberdeen Group)

Poor attribution costs the average $10M-$50M business between $400K and $2M per year in misallocated marketing spend.

Forrester

Email Attribution

25. Email marketing generates an average of $36 for every $1 spent but only when properly attributed (Data & Marketing Association / Litmus Email Marketing ROI Study 2022)

26. 68% of email conversions are misattributed to the last digital touchpoint (typically paid search), not to email (Epsilon Research)

27. Email attribution is considered the most difficult channel to measure accurately by 47% of marketers (HubSpot)

28. Companies that properly attribute email as a nurture channel (vs. direct response) see 33% higher email-influenced revenue (Salesforce State of Email)

Offline and Cross-Channel Attribution

29. 85% of customer journeys involve both online and offline touchpoints but fewer than 12% of companies have offline-to-online attribution in place (Forrester)

30. Phone call conversions are untracked by 73% of businesses that generate sales calls from digital ads (CallRail / BrightLocal Local Marketing Report)

31. The average B2B deal has 6.8 stakeholders involved in the decision making single-source attribution structurally impossible (Gartner)

32. Companies using revenue attribution (tying specific revenue dollars to marketing activities) outperform those using activity-based metrics by 38% in revenue growth (SiriusDecisions / Forrester)

Marketing Technology and Data Infrastructure

33. The average marketing technology stack has grown from 150 tools in 2011 to over 11,000 tools in 2023 creating massive data fragmentation (Chiefmartec Marketing Technology Landscape)

34. Only 29% of companies have their marketing and sales data in a unified system (Salesforce State of Marketing)

35. Data silos cost companies an average of 12-15% of total revenue annually in missed opportunities and inefficiency (Harvard Business Review / Gartner)

36. 44% of CRM data degrades by 20-25% each year due to poor data hygiene undermining attribution accuracy at the source (Gartner)

37. Companies with unified revenue data are 2.8x more likely to significantly exceed their revenue targets (Salesforce State of Sales)

38. Businesses investing in data infrastructure for attribution see an average 5:1 return within 18 months (Forrester TEI Analysis)

If your attribution is off, every budget decision you've made is based on the wrong data.
